can you explain it in more details? 3 Preprocessing: intensity normalization
Each volume V is loaded and reduced to a normalized intensity field on a fixed G × G × G grid (we
use G = 44).
1. Load NIfTI; if 4D keep the first channel; replace NaN/Inf with 0.
2. Foreground mask M = {|V | > 10−6}.
3. Robust window: lo, hi = the 1st and 99th percentiles of V over M.
4. Clip-and-scale: V ← clip
(V − lo)/(hi − lo), 0, 1

, and set background (¬M) to 0.
5. Resample V onto the G3 grid by trilinear interpolation (scipy.ndimage.map coordinates,
order 1).
This makes intensities comparable across scans of differing dynamic range, and the fixed grid
makes every volume a vector of the same length G3
.
